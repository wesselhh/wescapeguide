Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 28, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how your personal information is collected, used, and shared when you visit or interact with Wes | Cape Guide. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring that your personal information is handled securely and in compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) in South Africa and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Information We Collect

We collect information to provide a better experience and service to our clients and website visitors. This includes:

  • Information You Provide Voluntarily: When you fill out a contact or enquiry form, request an itinerary, or communicate with us directly, we collect details such as your name, email address, phone number, and any travel preferences or message content you share.
  • Automatically Collected Information: When you navigate the site, we automatically log specific technical details about your device and session, including your IP address, browser type, referring URLs, pages viewed, and the dates/times of your interactions.
  • Cookies and Tracking Technologies: We utilize cookies to understand site performance, analyze traffic, and enhance user navigation. You can easily adjust your browser preferences to disable cookies, though some interactive elements of the site may lose functionality.

2. How We Use Your Information

Your information allows us to run smoothly and craft better experiences. Specifically, we process it:

  • To respond directly to your enquiries, customize private itineraries, and manage tour bookings.
  • To monitor, analyze, and safely optimize website stability and design.
  • To communicate essential trip logistics, or occasional updates if you have explicitly opted in. You retain the right to unsubscribe instantly at any time.
  • To comply fully with statutory legal obligations or protect our operational rights.

3. Sharing Your Personal Information

We respect your data and do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We may disclose components of your information only under the following framework:

  • Service Providers: We share data with trusted tech partners who assist in maintaining our digital ecosystem (such as web hosting nodes, data analytics suites, or email dispatch systems), strictly governed under strict confidentiality parameters.
  • Legal Mandates: We may release details if explicitly required by law, official state audits, or formal regulatory requests from judicial frameworks.

4. International Data Transfers

Because the modern cloud ecosystem spans borders, your information may occasionally cross international data lanes for storage or hosting optimization. By choosing to interface with our website, you acknowledge and consent to these protected infrastructure paths.

5. Security of Your Data

We enforce modern organizational and technical firewalls designed to mitigate unauthorized access, disclosure, or data loss. That being said, no online transmission framework or cryptographic layer is completely impenetrable, and we cannot promise total absolute security across web pathways.

6. Your Legal Rights

Under both POPIA and GDPR frameworks, you maintain complete sovereignty over your information profile. Your capabilities include the right:

  • To request clear visibility over all data items we hold relative to your identity.
  • To correct, patch, or complete elements of data that are inaccurate or outdated.
  • To invoke data erasure ("the right to be forgotten") when those data files are no longer required for mandatory booking context.
  • To instantly object to any processing systems utilized for automated marketing or outreach campaigns.
  • To lodge a complaint regarding data mismanagement with the South African Information Regulator or your local regional authority.

7. Third-Party Links

This layout environment may point out or link directly to remote platforms (like maps or specific destination sights). We have no authority over the tracking architectures or underlying code rules of outside spaces, and we are not liable for their external behaviors.
